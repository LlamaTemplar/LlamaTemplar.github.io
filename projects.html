To The Light

To The Light is a 2D game based on the experience of waking up, as a child, from a nightmare and running through the dark to your parents room. I choose this experience because it’s one of the few childhood memories that really stand out for me. The most distinctive parts of my memory was turning on the lights as I went to my parents room. I turned that into the core mechanics of To The Light. The player must travel through the game by turning on the room and hallway lights in order to avoid the monster. The monster can threaten the player by turning off any light that the player is not in. Areas of safety change dramatically and the player must use timing and long term planning to complete each level.

NetGalaxy

Explore new worlds and solve problems along the way in NetGalaxy. The interactive piece challenges the user’s determination to never give up. The events in NetGalaxy present an issue, a set of items that need to be addressed, and a text box. It is up to the user to figure out how they will solve the problem, the only requirement is that they include all the items that need to be addressed. This experience was based on my fear of never being good enough for a programming job. I consider myself an experienced programmer, however, the job descriptions still haunt me. As I was working on NetGalaxy, I realized that my willingness to fight for what I wanted matter the most.

Tezca: Hidden Below

Tezca: Hidden Below is a first person horror game currently being developed in Unity3d. You play as Petra, a mexican soldier who falls into a hidden atez temple. Petra must unlock the puzzle to escape the darkness while avoiding a mysterious but deadly spirit. I’m one of 3 programmers and I’m responsible for programming the player as well as designing the levels. The whole team consists of 17 SJSU students and alumni.

Haunted Dimension

Haunted Dimension is a 3D render projected in the cafe of the Hammer Theatre in San Jose. This piece was part of the Cafe Afterlife projection mapping event that included other student work. The event was hosted by the CADRE Student Organization on November 1st, 2019.

The 3D render is an erie countryside landscape that peaks the viewer's curiosity. The diamond portal and the mysterious two figures convey a secret meeting.

Grungeon

Grungeon was one of the 1,040 submissions for itch.io's biggest game jam of 2018, Game Maker's Toolkit Jam 2018

This game was created by me and Thomas Zakharzhevskiy using Unity3d. The game consistent of procedural generated levels and three different enemies that were programmed by Thomas. All the visuals, animations, and UI were all created by me. In order to win the game, you had to traverse up the dungeon to find the key to unlock the escape door while being attacked by goblins.

Grungeon is now on display at the KALEID gallery in downtown San Jose.

A Way of Life

Everyone relies only on a couple companies in their everyday life. The apps that Facebook, Amazon, and Google offer have become irreplaceable to us. It’s hard to imagine what life would be like with these Silicon Valley tech companies, however this discussion has been growing. Institutions are catching on to the power that the companies have on our lives and the economy.

With my video installation, I wanted to tie in religion with the big tech companies. Facebook, Amazon, and Google are the subjects of this piece because they’re the most influential companies in our everyday lives. The church is meant to convey the grand scale of the three companies. I wanted the viewer to think about the power the three companies have and how they would live without them.

Data Pal

It's well known that apps in our phone collect data about us, but do you know how much data is being collected and how often? This was the question that inspired the creation of this video along with this article. Tech companies are usually very vague when describing how they use your data and I'm not surprised. Take a moment to think about what you do on the internet and try to imagine how companies like Google might use those footprints you leave behind. Sure they record what ads you click on or what you search, but what is stopping them from tracking your patterns and behaviors? What's stopping them from checking your location ten times a minute and then comparing the data you browse on the internet to the places you travel to?
